What is a Gaussian Beam?

A Gaussian beam is the fundamental transverse electromagnetic mode (TEM‚ÇÄ‚ÇÄ) produced by most lasers. Unlike geometric ray optics ‚Äî which treats light as infinitely thin rays ‚Äî a Gaussian beam has a smooth, bell-shaped intensity profile described by:

where r is the radial distance from the beam axis and w(z) is the beam radius at position z. The beam has a finite width, a well-defined focus (the beam waist), and spreads due to diffraction as it propagates.

This diffraction-based description is essential for laser optics. Geometric ray optics predicts that a perfect lens focuses light to an infinitely small point ‚Äî Gaussian beam theory correctly predicts the actual finite spot size.
